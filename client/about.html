Bringing the roots of Aviation Safety to American Healthcare!

NearMiss Health is the dream of Clinical experts, Biostatisticians, Healthcare Administrators, and an Air Force Pilot and Crash Investigator. It seeks to bring the safety gains of easy, online, anonymous reporting of near misses realized by US Commercial aviation to healthcare.

Wenjie Bao is a Senior Project Manager at Epidemico, where he works at the intersection of management, public health, and data science. He has worked as a language analyst at Boston Children’s Hospital, where he helped optimize Chinese language search algorithms, and at the Chinese Center for Disease Control and Prevention, where he was an epidemiologist tracking hospital-born pathogen outbreaks. He holds a MSc in Preventive Medicine, Epidemiology and Public Health from Harvard University, an MSc in Biochemistry from George Washington University, and a B.Eng in Bioengineering from Southern Yangtze University.

Danielle Kim is an Administrative Fellow at the Harvard University Health Service, where she does work in outpatient safety. She holds an HMSA in Health Management and Policy, a Masters in Applied Statistics, A BS in Economics, and a BS in applied Mathematics from the University of Michigan.

Glenn Oetttinger, PharmD, is a Clinical Assistant Professor at Thomas Jefferson University and an Emergency Department Pharmacy Specialist at Thomas Jefferson University Hospital, where he is actively engaged as a Population Health Change Agent and Lean Management Leader. He holds a Doctorate of Pharmacy degree from University of the Sciences in Philadelphia.

Miranda Peeples is a Senior analyst at Analysis Group in Boston, where she does statistical programming and population health research. She holds a degree in Economics from Yale University.

Alex Rich, MBA is a student at the Yale School of Public Health studying the intersection of healthcare management and technology. He is a former Major and Senior Pilot in US Air Force Special Operations command. He was trained as an instructor pilot and Flight Safety Officer and spent several years helping manage the safety and mishap investigation program of a Wing operating $3.6 billion in KC-135 aircraft. He holds a B.S. in Aerospace Engineering from Virginia Tech, an MBA from Oklahoma State University, and expects to finish an MPH at Yale University in May of 2017.

Jean Zheng, PhD is the Engineering Director at the Yale Center for Biomedical and Interventional Technology. She has managed 50+ clinical-led medical device projects and was the catalyst a lead of the first Healthcare Hackathons at Yale University. She led a team that won the Brigham Innovation Hub Prize at MIT Hacking Medicine’s Brigham & Women’s Hospital Healthcare Hackathon in 2014. She also won the National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship award 2008-2011. In addition to bringing a wealth of experience, perspective and connections, Jean holds an M.S./PH.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Yale University and a BS in Mechanical Engineering from MIT.
