Bringing the roots of Aviation Safety to American Healthcare!

Alex Rich is a former Major and Senior Pilot in US Air Force Special Operations command. He was trained as an Instructor Pilot, Flight Safety Officer, and Crash Investigator and spent several years helping manage the safety and mishap investigation program of a Wing operating $3.6 billion in KC-135 aircraft. Alex was a Finalist at the MIT Hacking Medicine Grand Hack in April 2016. He holds a B.S. in Aerospace Engineering from Virginia Tech, an MBA from Oklahoma State University, and is completing MPH at Yale University in May of 2017.

Jean Zheng, PhD is the Engineering Director at the Yale Center for Biomedical and Interventional Technology. She has managed 50+ clinical-led medical device projects and was the catalyst a lead of the first Healthcare Hackathons at Yale University. She led a team that won the Brigham Innovation Hub Prize at MIT Hacking Medicine’s Brigham & Women’s Hospital Healthcare Hackathon in 2014. She also won the National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship award 2008-2011. In addition to bringing a wealth of experience, perspective and connections, Jean holds an M.S./PH.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Yale University and a BS in Mechanical Engineering from MIT.

Dave McCredie is a former Captain and Evaluator Combat Systems Operator in Air Force Special Operations Command. He is completing an MPH in Healthcare Management at the University of Michigan in May 2017 and spent the summer of 2016 as a business analyst at United Health. He holds a BS in Chemistry from the University of Texas, Austin and an Masters in International Affairs from the University of Oklahoma.

John Cronin has been a nursing manager at 3 of the 5 largest trauma centers in the country. He is currently an internal consultant doing strategy and cost management work at a major academic medical center. In 2016 he was a finalist in the Cleveland Clinic Case Competition. He holds a BS in Nursing from The Catholic University of America and an MPH in Healthcare Management from Yale University.
